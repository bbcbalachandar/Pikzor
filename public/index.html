FAQ

Common questions

An OG image (Open Graph image) is the preview image that appears when you share a link on Twitter, LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage, and other platforms. Without one, you get a gray box. With a good one, you get a branded card that drives clicks. The <meta property="og:image"> tag in your page's <head> tells social platforms which image to use.
About 2 minutes. Sign up, upload your logo, pick a color and template, and you'll get a URL. Paste it into your site's <head> tag as an og:image meta tag and you're done. If you use Next.js, WordPress, or Ghost, we have copy-paste snippets in the docs.
Barely. You need to be able to add a line to your site's HTML — something like <meta property="og:image" content="https://pikzor.com/og/t_xK9mP2?title={{title}}">. If you can copy-paste HTML, you can set this up. Most CMS platforms (WordPress, Ghost, Webflow, Framer) have a field for this in settings.
Any platform that respects the Open Graph protocol — which is basically all of them. Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Facebook, Slack, Discord, iMessage, WhatsApp, Telegram, Notion, and more. If a platform shows link previews, it reads og:image. Pikzor images also include twitter:card support for Twitter's large card format.
